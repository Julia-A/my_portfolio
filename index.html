About Me.

I graduated with first-class honors in Computer Information Systems from Babcock University—tech fundamentals down, but more importantly, I learned how to think like a builder. I’m a backend developer who enjoys making systems that are clean under the hood and calm under pressure.

I worked with PoolUp, a startup in the transport space, where I helped build out their backend infrastructure, from APIs to database models. Startups teach you fast: move quick, write code that lasts, and solve problems nobody’s solved yet.

Outside of official roles, I’ve built and shipped several projects you’ll find below—each one a result of curiosity meeting execution. I care about performance, clean structure, and writing code that doesn’t just work today but still makes sense months from now.

Most days, you’ll find me coding to lofi beats in a dimly lit room.
